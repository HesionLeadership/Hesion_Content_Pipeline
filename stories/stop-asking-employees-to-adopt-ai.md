# Stop Asking Employees to Adopt AI

**Source:** Inc  
**Published:** Mon, 13 Jul 2026 07:42:41 -0400  
**URL:** [https://www.inc.com/fast-company-2/stop-asking-employees-to-adopt-ai/91373105](https://www.inc.com/fast-company-2/stop-asking-employees-to-adopt-ai/91373105)

## Summary
The article argues that mandating AI adoption among employees is counterproductive; instead, leaders should provide a compelling vision, make work more meaningful, and ensure AI tools are so seamlessly integrated that adoption becomes the path of least resistance.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This reflects core principles of self-determination theory—employees adopt new behaviors when they feel autonomy, competence, and relatedness rather than external pressure. Mandates undermine intrinsic motivation and can trigger psychological reactance, while purpose-driven framing and reduced friction align with sustained behavior change.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should stop framing AI as a compliance requirement and instead invest in articulating how AI transforms the nature of work toward more meaningful tasks, while reducing adoption barriers—treating technology change as a job design and motivation challenge rather than a policy enforcement issue.
