# Banks accused of pushing customers away from basic accounts

**Source:** BBC Business  
**Published:** Mon, 06 Jul 2026 23:29:34 GMT  
**URL:** [https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/cgl33dyk7y9o?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/cgl33dyk7y9o?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss)

## Summary
UK financial regulator has found that major banks are failing vulnerable customers by steering them away from basic bank accounts they are entitled to, instead pushing them toward fee-paying alternatives.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This reflects an organizational culture and incentive structure problem where frontline employees may be driven by sales targets or institutional norms to prioritize revenue over customer welfare, highlighting misalignment between corporate values and actual behavior.
