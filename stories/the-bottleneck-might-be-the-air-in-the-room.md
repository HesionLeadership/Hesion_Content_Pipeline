# The bottleneck might be the air in the room

**Source:** Hacker News  
**Published:** Sat, 04 Jul 2026 06:32:28 +0000  
**URL:** [https://blog.mikebowler.ca/2026/07/03/co2-and-decision-making/](https://blog.mikebowler.ca/2026/07/03/co2-and-decision-making/)

## Summary
The article discusses how poor indoor air quality (high CO2 levels) in meeting rooms and offices can impair cognitive performance, acting as a hidden bottleneck for knowledge workers and decision-making.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Environmental factors like air quality directly impact cognitive function, decision-making quality, and team performance—a critical but overlooked aspect of workplace design and organizational effectiveness.
