# You Outsourced the AI—but You Still Own the Risk

**Source:** HBR  
**Published:** 2026-07-09T12:25:35Z  
**URL:** [http://feeds.hbr.org/2026/07/you-outsourced-the-ai-but-you-still-own-the-risk](http://feeds.hbr.org/2026/07/you-outsourced-the-ai-but-you-still-own-the-risk)

## Summary
Organizations that outsource AI tools to third-party vendors remain legally and regulatorily liable when those tools discriminate, mishandle data, or harm customers.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This highlights a critical leadership accountability gap: delegating technical work to vendors can create a psychological diffusion of responsibility, where leaders assume outsourcing transfers not just the task but also the ownership of outcomes and ethical obligations.

## Strength Score
**7/10** — The story directly addresses organizational accountability structures, decision-making around vendor relationships, and the leadership challenge of maintaining responsibility across organizational boundaries. The diffusion of responsibility and principal-agent dynamics are well-established org psych concepts. It's relevant to how leaders structure governance, risk ownership, and organizational culture around emerging technology adoption.
