# Redefine What ‘Professionalism’ Means

**Source:** MIT Sloan Review  
**Published:** Thu, 25 Jun 2026 11:00:39 +0000

## Summary
The article examines how traditional notions of 'professionalism' vary across workplaces and industries, questioning whether shared norms around communication style, punctuality, and meeting etiquette need to be redefined for modern, diverse, and hybrid work environments.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Professionalism norms function as implicit organizational culture artifacts that can either foster inclusion or inadvertently marginalize individuals whose backgrounds or work styles differ from dominant group expectations. Redefining these norms requires deliberate negotiation of shared expectations, touching on psychological safety and cultural inclusivity.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should proactively facilitate explicit conversations about workplace norms rather than assuming a single standard of 'professionalism'—co-creating expectations with diverse teams reduces ambiguity, increases belonging, and prevents exclusionary practices that masquerade as professional standards.
