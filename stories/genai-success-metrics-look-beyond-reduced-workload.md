# GenAI Success Metrics: Look Beyond Reduced Workload

**Source:** MIT Sloan Review  
**Published:** Wed, 08 Jul 2026 11:00:17 +0000  
**URL:** [https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/genai-success-metrics-look-beyond-reduced-workload/](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/genai-success-metrics-look-beyond-reduced-workload/)

## Summary
A four-year observational study at a large U.S. public university found that introducing generative AI tools did not reduce workload or staffing levels, suggesting organizations should measure GenAI success through metrics beyond simple workload reduction.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
When organizations adopt AI tools expecting efficiency gains measured solely by reduced headcount or hours, they miss how work actually transforms—tasks may shift in complexity, cognitive demand, or quality rather than quantity. This reflects the broader challenge of techno-change management where misaligned success metrics undermine adoption and morale.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should define GenAI success metrics that capture work quality, task complexity shifts, employee skill development, and decision-making improvement rather than defaulting to headcount reduction or hours saved—misaligned metrics risk demoralizing staff and misrepresenting the technology's true organizational impact.
