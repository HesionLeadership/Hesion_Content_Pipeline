## Summary
Fidji Simo, OpenAI's No. 2 executive, is stepping down from her full-time role after an extended medical leave, creating a leadership vacuum as the company prepares for a potential IPO and intensifies its enterprise competition with Anthropic.

The departure of a second-in-command during a critical strategic inflection point highlights succession planning risks and the organizational fragility that emerges when key leadership roles lack redundancy, especially in hypergrowth companies racing toward major milestones.
