# 'Less than 10% sheep': How millions may have unknowingly eaten goat, skin and fat kebabs

**Source:** BBC Business  
**Published:** Fri, 03 Jul 2026 05:14:17 GMT  
**URL:** [https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/ce95y1zlzyxo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/ce95y1zlzyxo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss)

## Summary
An investigation revealed that millions of consumers may have unknowingly eaten kebabs labeled as lamb that were actually made primarily from goat meat, skin, and fat, with less than 10% actual sheep content.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This story raises significant issues around supply chain ethics, organizational integrity, and the culture of deception within food production businesses—where leadership failures and lack of accountability enable systematic fraud against consumers and legitimate competitors.
