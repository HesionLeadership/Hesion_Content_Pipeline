# Abel goes his own way with new Berkshire investments, including billions for AI

**Source:** CNBC CEO  
**Published:** Sat, 06 Jun 2026 12:31:02 GMT  
**URL:** [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/06/abel-goes-his-own-way-with-new-berkshire-investments-including-billions-for-ai.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/06/abel-goes-his-own-way-with-new-berkshire-investments-including-billions-for-ai.html)

## Summary
New Berkshire Hathaway CEO Greg Abel is making his first major independent investment decisions, including billions in AI, as Warren Buffett publicly endorses his successor's early moves.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This is a textbook CEO succession case study: how a legendary founder publicly validates and empowers his successor, and how the new leader establishes autonomy and distinct strategic identity while operating under the shadow of an iconic predecessor.

## Strength Score
**8/10** — This story directly addresses leadership succession dynamics, one of the most studied topics in organizational psychology. The interplay between Buffett's endorsement ('launched'), Abel's need to differentiate his investment philosophy (AI vs. Buffett's tech aversion), and the organizational challenge of transitioning from a founder-CEO who defined the company's identity for 60 years makes this rich with org psych relevance—covering successor legitimacy, identity transition, and stakeholder confidence management.

## Original Story Summary
Warren Buffett tells CNBC's Becky Quick new Berkshire Hathaway CEO Greg Abel has "launched" with his first major deal.
