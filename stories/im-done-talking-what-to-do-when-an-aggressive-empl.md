## Summary
The article addresses how managers should handle aggressive employee behavior—such as cursing, yelling at clients, and physical outbursts—when soft management approaches have failed and the situation demands decisive action.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This speaks directly to the challenge of managing counterproductive work behaviors (CWBs) and the limits of participative or lenient management styles when employees exhibit workplace aggression. It highlights the tension between relational leadership and the need for firm boundary enforcement to protect team psychological safety.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should ensure managers are trained and empowered to escalate interventions decisively when employee behavior crosses into aggression—progressive discipline frameworks must include clear thresholds where coaching gives way to formal consequences, protecting both organizational culture and other employees' well-being.
