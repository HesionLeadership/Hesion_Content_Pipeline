# Leadership’s Blind Spot in the Age of AI

**Source:** MIT Sloan Review  
**Published:** Tue, 07 Jul 2026 11:00:17 +0000  
**URL:** [https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/leaderships-blind-spot-in-the-age-of-ai/](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/leaderships-blind-spot-in-the-age-of-ai/)

## Summary
The article explores how AI is exposing a fundamental blind spot in leadership thinking, drawing on Heidegger's philosophy to argue that leaders are still not engaging in deep, reflective thinking about the nature of intelligence and decision-making in the age of AI.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Leaders face a metacognitive challenge: the tendency to adopt AI tools without critically examining assumptions about what constitutes thinking, judgment, and decision-making in organizations. This blind spot risks automating processes without understanding the cognitive and relational dimensions that drive organizational effectiveness.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should invest in building organizational capacity for reflective, critical thinking—not just AI technical skills. Leaders need structured opportunities to examine their assumptions about intelligence, automation, and human judgment before deploying AI at scale, or they risk optimizing for efficiency while eroding the deeper thinking that drives strategic advantage.
