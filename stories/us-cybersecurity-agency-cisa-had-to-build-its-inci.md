# US cybersecurity agency CISA had to build its incident playbook during the incident, agency reveals

**Source:** TechCrunch  
**Published:** Sat, 11 Jul 2026 01:01:28 +0000  
**URL:** [https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/us-cyber-agency-cisa-had-to-build-its-incident-playbook-during-the-incident-agency-reveals/](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/us-cyber-agency-cisa-had-to-build-its-incident-playbook-during-the-incident-agency-reveals/)

## Summary
CISA, the US cybersecurity agency, revealed it had to build its incident response playbook during an actual security incident, after a contractor employee uploaded exposed passwords to a public GitHub repository.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This highlights a significant organizational preparedness failure—the agency tasked with guiding others on cybersecurity incident response lacked its own internal playbook, revealing gaps in organizational learning, contingency planning, and the challenge of practicing what you preach when institutional processes haven't kept pace with the organization's external mandate.

## Strength Score
**7/10** — This story has strong organizational psychology relevance: it illustrates institutional unpreparedness, the gap between espoused organizational values and actual practice (a classic org psych concept), contractor oversight challenges, and the dynamics of building crisis management capabilities under pressure. It also touches on organizational identity—how an agency whose mission is incident response preparedness can itself lack that preparedness internally.

## Original Story Summary
Independent cybersecurity journalist Brian Krebs reported in May that a security researcher with cyber firm GitGuardian alerted him to reams of exposed passwords stored in a publicly accessible GitHub repository, which an employee of a CISA contractor had uploaded.
