## Summary
Bending Spoons surged 40% on its first day of trading, having grown rapidly through a strategy of acquiring and revamping legacy tech brands like Evernote, Meetup, Vimeo, and AOL.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Bending Spoons' acquisition-and-turnaround model raises compelling questions about organizational integration, cultural transformation of legacy workforces, and the leadership challenge of imposing operational discipline on previously independent brands—a classic post-M&A identity and change management challenge.

## Strength Score
**7/10** — The serial acquisition strategy inherently involves significant organizational psychology dynamics: integrating diverse company cultures, managing workforce transitions (Bending Spoons is known for significant layoffs post-acquisition), maintaining motivation during turnarounds, and the leadership philosophy of treating software products as operational assets rather than innovation vehicles. These are substantive org psych themes even if the article itself focuses more on financial performance.

## Original Story Summary
