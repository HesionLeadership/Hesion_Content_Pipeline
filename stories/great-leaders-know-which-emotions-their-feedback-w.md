## Summary
The article explores how effective leaders anticipate and manage the diverse emotional reactions employees have to feedback, offering six techniques to ensure feedback is received constructively.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Individual differences in emotional reactivity and feedback orientation significantly affect how employees process and act on performance feedback. Leaders who calibrate their delivery based on these differences can improve receptivity and developmental outcomes.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should train managers to assess individual employees' emotional tendencies and feedback preferences before delivering evaluations, rather than using a one-size-fits-all approach. Building emotional intelligence in managers directly improves the effectiveness of performance conversations and reduces defensive reactions.
