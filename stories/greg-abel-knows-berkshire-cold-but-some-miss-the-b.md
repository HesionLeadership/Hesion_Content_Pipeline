## Summary
Greg Abel led his first Berkshire Hathaway annual shareholders meeting as CEO, receiving decent but not spectacular reviews as he steps into Warren Buffett's iconic role.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This is a textbook case of leadership succession dynamics — how a new leader navigates the shadow of a legendary predecessor, manages stakeholder expectations, and establishes legitimacy in a role defined by charismatic authority rather than formal authority alone.

## Strength Score
**8/10** — CEO succession at an iconic company like Berkshire Hathaway, especially following a once-in-a-generation charismatic leader like Buffett, is a rich organizational psychology topic. It touches on charismatic vs. rational-legal authority (Weber), successor legitimacy, identity continuity of the organization, and how stakeholders psychologically process the transition from a founder-like figure. The 'some miss the Buffett magic' framing directly highlights the challenge of succeeding a transformational leader.
