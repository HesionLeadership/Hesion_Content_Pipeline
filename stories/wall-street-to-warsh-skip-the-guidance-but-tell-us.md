# Wall Street to Warsh: Skip the Guidance, But Tell Us What You Think About the Economy

**Source:** Bloomberg  
**Published:** Mon, 13 Jul 2026 10:00:00 GMT  
**URL:** [https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/wall-street-to-fed-s-warsh-skip-the-guidance-tell-us-what-you-think](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/wall-street-to-fed-s-warsh-skip-the-guidance-tell-us-what-you-think)

## Summary
Wall Street critics argue that Federal Reserve Chairman Kevin Warsh's commitment to eliminating forward guidance has come at the cost of transparency about his economic thinking and decision-making framework, leaving markets uncertain about how he would respond to economic challenges.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This highlights a core leadership communication tension: in seeking to reduce one form of signaling (forward guidance), Warsh has inadvertently created a transparency vacuum about his cognitive framework and decision-making style, undermining stakeholder trust and predictability—key elements of effective organizational leadership.

## Original Story Summary
In his zeal to avoid signaling where interest rates are headed, Federal Reserve Chairman Kevin Warsh has obscured something else that’s crucial to investors, analysts and other policymakers: How he would react when challenged by the economy.
