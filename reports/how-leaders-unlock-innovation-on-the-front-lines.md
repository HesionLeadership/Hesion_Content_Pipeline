## Summary
The article explores how leaders can balance operational demands with fostering innovation at the frontline level, noting that work overload and stress constrain employees' ideation capacity and creative thinking.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Cognitive load from operational pressures creates a bottleneck that suppresses creative ideation among frontline employees. Leaders who actively manage workload and create psychological space for experimentation can unlock innovation without sacrificing operational performance.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should design work systems that deliberately protect cognitive bandwidth for innovation—through workload management, structured ideation time, and reducing unnecessary operational friction—rather than expecting creativity to emerge spontaneously from already-overburdened teams.
