# Morning Briefing — July 13, 2026

## Top Story
**The 'Job-Hugging' Trend Is Cracking: Why Half of Employees Are Now Looking to Leave** (Inc)

This is the story CHROs need to hear right now, and many aren't ready for it. The "frozen" labor market gave leaders a false sense of security — low turnover looked like loyalty, but it was actually suppressed flight risk. With 50% of workers now actively looking, organizations that coasted on fear-based retention are about to pay the price. Pete, this is the kind of insight that makes a CHRO lean forward in a meeting — it reframes a metric they thought was good news (low turnover) as a lagging indicator of a coming crisis. The window for re-engagement is closing.

## Best LinkedIn Post Candidate
**Leaders at All Levels: How DBS Bank Makes Everyone an Innovator** (MIT Sloan Review)

This is a clean, concrete, shareable example — not theory, not opinion, but a real company that made innovation 20% of every performance review. LinkedIn audiences love a specific mechanism they can steal. **Suggested hook:** "Most companies say they want innovation. DBS Bank actually put a number on it — and it changed everything."

## Best Newsletter Candidate
**What 60 Years of Data Reveals About How Men and Women Experience Leadership** (HBR)

This one deserves the long-form treatment. Sixty years of longitudinal data is rare and compelling, and the core finding — that men systematically underestimate the barriers women face — is the kind of insight that challenges leaders who believe their organizations are already meritocratic. Pete can unpack the perceptual asymmetry concept, connect it to organizational justice theory, and make the case that representation metrics alone are insufficient. There's also a practical thread to pull: if men in leadership don't see the problem, they'll resist or underinvest in the solution, which explains why so many equity initiatives stall. This has enough depth for 800+ words and directly serves the CHRO audience.

## Best Workshop/Keynote Example
**Great Leaders Know Which Emotions Their Feedback Will Trigger** (HBR)

This is a perfect live-session illustration. Every leader in the room has given feedback that landed badly, and most have never stopped to ask why the same message works with one person and backfires with another. Pete could use this to teach the concept of feedback orientation and emotional appraisal differences — then run a quick exercise where participants map their team members' likely emotional reactions to a sample piece of critical feedback. It makes the abstract concrete and gives leaders something they can use Monday morning. Works beautifully in a module on coaching, difficult conversations, or emotional intelligence in leadership.

- **Leadership's Blind Spot in the Age of AI** (MIT Sloan) — Solid conceptual piece on metacognition and automation complacency; pairs well with the GenAI metrics story if Pete wants to build an AI-and-leadership content series.
