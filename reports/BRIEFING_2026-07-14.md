# Morning Briefing — July 14, 2026

## Top Story
**Gallup CEO says colonizing Mars may be closer than fixing today's 'broken' workplace—where disengagement levels are as high as 2020** (Fortune). Jon Clifton's provocation is a direct indictment of how most organizations approach engagement: decades of survey programs, wellness perks, and culture decks have produced exactly zero net progress. For Pete's CHRO and COO audience, this is the uncomfortable mirror — if four in five workers are disengaged despite everything you've invested, the problem isn't effort, it's approach. The prescription is structural: manager quality, job redesign for autonomy and meaning, and real accountability for engagement outcomes at every level of leadership.

## Best LinkedIn Post Candidate
**The 'Job-Hugging' Trend Is Cracking: Why Half of Employees Are Now Looking to Leave** (Inc). This is a ticking clock story — urgency plus counterintuitive insight — which is LinkedIn gold. CHROs who saw low turnover the past two years and congratulated themselves are about to get a rude awakening. **Suggested hook:** "Your best retention year might have been your biggest warning sign. Here's why."

## Best Newsletter Candidate
**What 60 Years of Data Reveals About How Men and Women Experience Leadership** (HBR). This one has the depth Pete needs for a 500+ word treatment. Sixty years of longitudinal data gives real analytical weight, and the core finding — that men systematically underestimate the barriers women face, creating perceptual asymmetries around fairness and meritocracy — is something Pete can unpack through organizational justice theory, in-group bias, and the gap between representation metrics and lived experience. It's a piece that lets Pete challenge CHROs to measure what they're currently assuming: that improved headcount numbers mean the culture has actually shifted.

## Best Workshop/Keynote Example
**Leaders at All Levels: How DBS Bank Makes Everyone an Innovator** (MIT Sloan Review). This is a clean, specific, nameable case study that illustrates the gap between cultural aspiration and structural execution. DBS didn't just say "we value innovation" — they made it 20% of every performance review, for every team, at every level. In a keynote on aligning systems to strategy, Pete can use this as the "before and after" — most organizations have the rhetoric, DBS built the mechanism. It's a concrete illustration of expectancy theory and goal-setting theory applied at enterprise scale.

## Stories Worth Watching
- **The Best Teams Know When to Work Alone** (HBR) — Strong counternarrative to collaboration overload; useful for any work design conversation with COOs rethinking hybrid structures.
- **Great Leaders Know Which Emotions Their Feedback Will Trigger** (HBR) — Practical and relevant for manager development programs; good supporting material but not novel enough to lead with.
- **GenAI Success Metrics: Look Beyond Reduced Workload** (MIT Sloan Review) — The four-year study finding that GenAI didn't reduce workload is a genuinely surprising data point; worth referencing when clients are over-indexing on AI efficiency promises.
- **Most Leaders Track Employee Turnover. Few Know What It's Actually Costing Them** (Inc) — Pairs well with the job-hugging story; useful framing for retention investment conversations but the argument itself isn't new.

## Skip These
- **The Office Amenity That Actually Improves Teamwork** (HBR) — Clickbait framing around a single amenity; too narrow and tactical for Pete's strategic audience.
- **The Best Leaders Aren't Replacing Employees With AI, They're Empowering Them** (Inc) — Correct but generic; the empowerment-over-replacement argument has been made a thousand times this year without new data or frameworks to differentiate Pete's voice.
