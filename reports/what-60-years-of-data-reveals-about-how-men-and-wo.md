# What 60 Years of Data Reveals About How Men and Women Experience Leadership

**Source:** HBR  
**Published:** 2026-07-10T12:05:43Z  
**URL:** [http://feeds.hbr.org/2026/07/what-60-years-of-data-reveals-about-how-men-and-women-experience-leadership](http://feeds.hbr.org/2026/07/what-60-years-of-data-reveals-about-how-men-and-women-experience-leadership)

## Summary
A 60-year longitudinal analysis reveals that while attitudes toward women in leadership have improved significantly, persistent gender gaps remain in how men and women perceive evaluation fairness, performance standards, and advancement opportunities.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
The study highlights enduring perceptual asymmetries between men and women regarding organizational justice and meritocracy—men consistently underestimate the barriers women face, which has direct implications for how equity interventions are designed and received.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should not assume that improving representation metrics alone signals cultural progress; they must actively measure and address gender differences in perceived fairness of evaluation systems, promotion criteria, and opportunity access, as these perceptual gaps drive disengagement and turnover among women leaders.
