# Morning Briefing — July 20, 2026

## Top Story
**Gallup CEO says colonizing Mars may be closer than fixing today's 'broken' workplace** (Fortune). Four in five workers globally are disengaged—back to 2020 levels—despite billions spent on engagement programs over the past decade. This is the number CHROs and COOs least want to hear but most need to sit with: the current playbook isn't working. The implication for Hesion's audience is stark—if your engagement strategy centers on surveys, perks, and annual town halls, you're treating symptoms while the disease metastasizes. The fix is upstream: manager capability, job design for autonomy and meaning, and real accountability for engagement outcomes at every level of leadership. This is a story that validates Pete's entire practice.

## Best LinkedIn Post Candidate
**Your company doesn't have a talent shortage. It has a talent visibility problem** (Fast Company). The 21-point gap between managers who think they know their people's skills and employees who agree is a perfect, shareable data point that reframes a hot-button issue. CHROs will stop scrolling for this.

**Hook:** "90% of managers say they know what their people are capable of. Only 69% of employees agree. That gap isn't a communication issue—it's an organizational design failure that's costing you your best people."

## Best Newsletter Candidate
**Women and men in the C-suite see two different workplaces** (Fast Company) + **What 60 years of data reveals about how men and women experience leadership** (HBR). These are essentially companion pieces drawing from the same longitudinal HBR dataset, and together they deserve a deep-dive newsletter. The org psych richness here is substantial: 'prove-it-again' bias, organizational sensemaking failures, perceptual asymmetry around meritocracy, and the gap between formal policy equality and experienced equality. Pete can unpack why representation metrics alone are insufficient, introduce the concept of perception audits segmented by demographic, and offer a practical framework for CHROs to diagnose and close fairness perception gaps. This is the kind of evidence-based, actionable analysis that positions Hesion as doing more than commentary.

## Best Workshop/Keynote Example
**Stripe is the most underrated—and influential—$159 billion company in the world** (Fast Company). This is a perfect case study for illustrating how psychological safety and high performance standards aren't opposites—they're multipliers. The specific practices are workshop-ready: rigorous writing to reduce political decision-making, leaders staying in operational pain rather than relying on filtered reports, "altitude shifting" as a hiring criterion, and mission framing that expands your talent pool. The 268 alumni founders stat is a powerful proof point for the concept that great cultures don't just retain talent—they become talent ecosystems. **Concept it illustrates:** Building cooperative rigor—high challenge + high support—as the engine of sustainable performance culture.
