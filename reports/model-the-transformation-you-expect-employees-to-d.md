# Model the Transformation You Expect Employees to Deliver

**Source:** HBR

## Summary
HBR article arguing that leaders must personally model the transformation behaviors they expect from employees, as employees follow leaders' actions rather than their words.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This directly addresses the behavioral integrity construct—the perceived alignment between a leader's words and actions—which research shows is a critical driver of employee trust, engagement, and willingness to adopt change. It also connects to social learning theory, where employees observe and emulate leader behavior as a primary mechanism for cultural transmission.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs driving organizational transformation should ensure leaders at every level are visibly practicing the new behaviors, processes, and mindsets they are asking employees to adopt. Transformation initiatives fail when leadership behavior signals 'do as I say, not as I do'—invest in leader behavior change first, then cascade expectations.
