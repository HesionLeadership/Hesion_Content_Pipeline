# Morning Briefing — July 21, 2026

## Top Story
**Gallup CEO says colonizing Mars may be closer than fixing today's 'broken' workplace** (Fortune). Four in five workers globally are disengaged — right back to 2020 levels — despite billions poured into engagement programs. This is the story Pete should be talking about this week because it's a direct indictment of how most organizations approach engagement: surveys without follow-through, perks instead of autonomy, and zero accountability for managers. For CHROs and COOs, this is a "your house is on fire and you're rearranging furniture" moment. Hesion's whole value proposition lives in this gap between what organizations are doing and what the evidence says they should be doing.

## Best LinkedIn Post Candidate
**Your company doesn't have a talent shortage. It has a talent visibility problem** (Fast Company). The 21-point gap between managers who think they know their people's skills (90%) and employees who agree (69%) is a punchy, counterintuitive stat that stops the scroll — and it reframes a complaint every CHRO is tired of hearing ("we can't find talent") into an internal systems problem they can actually fix. **Suggested hook:** "90% of managers say they understand their team's skills. Only 69% of employees agree. That gap is why you're hiring externally for roles someone on your team could already do."

## Best Newsletter Candidate
**Women and men in the C-suite see two different workplaces** (Fast Company) + **What 60 Years of Data Reveals About How Men and Women Experience Leadership** (HBR). These are effectively the same underlying research (the 60-year HBR longitudinal study) covered from two angles, and together they deserve a deep-dive newsletter. The org psych richness here is exceptional: organizational sensemaking failures, prove-it-again bias, perceptual asymmetry on meritocracy, and the dangerous assumption that policy equality equals experienced equality. Pete can unpack why male executives systematically underestimate the barriers their female peers face — and why that perceptual gap itself is the problem, not just the barriers. This is a 700-word piece that writes itself and positions Hesion squarely in the equity-as-organizational-effectiveness lane, not the compliance lane.

## Best Workshop/Keynote Example
**Stripe's culture of urgency without aggression** (Fast Company). This is a vivid, concrete case study for any talk on high-performance culture or psychological safety. The Collisons model urgency by doing the work alongside employees rather than demanding it from above. Stripe institutionalizes written memos to force clarity and reduce politics. They've produced 268 alumni founders — meaning the culture builds entrepreneurial capacity, not dependency. **Concept it illustrates:** You can have high standards AND psychological safety — they're not trade-offs, they're multipliers. Use this as the counterexample every time a leader says "we can't afford to be soft" or "psychological safety means lowering the bar."
