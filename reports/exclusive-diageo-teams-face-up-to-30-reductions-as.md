## Summary
Diageo's CEO, nicknamed 'Drastic Dave,' is implementing sweeping cost cuts that include up to 30% reductions in some teams, signaling aggressive restructuring at the global drinks giant.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Large-scale workforce reductions of this magnitude create significant survivor syndrome, erode psychological safety, and can trigger talent flight among high performers who have the most external options. The CEO's 'Drastic Dave' nickname suggests a leadership brand built around decisive cost-cutting, which can signal clarity of direction but also risks dehumanizing the change process.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs overseeing deep headcount reductions should proactively manage survivor guilt and anxiety through transparent communication, procedural justice in selection decisions, and visible investment in remaining employees—otherwise the 30% cut risks becoming a 50% capability loss as disengaged survivors disengage or leave voluntarily.
