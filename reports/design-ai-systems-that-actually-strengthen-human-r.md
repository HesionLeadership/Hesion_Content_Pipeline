## Summary
The article discusses strategies for designing AI systems that enhance rather than diminish human critical thinking, organizational agility, and innovation capacity.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
AI tools risk creating cognitive offloading and automation complacency, where employees stop engaging in deep reasoning. Designing AI to augment rather than replace human judgment directly impacts organizational learning, decision quality, and adaptive capacity.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should ensure AI implementations are designed to prompt critical thinking rather than passive acceptance—building systems that present options, challenge assumptions, and require human judgment, thereby preserving the cognitive skills essential for innovation and strategic agility.
