# I changed jobs 10 times in 10 years to get the career I wanted

**Source:** BBC Business  
**Published:** Tue, 14 Jul 2026 23:04:15 GMT  
**URL:** [https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/ce8kxgpe3kdo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/ce8kxgpe3kdo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss)

## Summary
The 'lily-padding' trend describes young professionals who change jobs frequently—up to 10 times in 10 years—as a deliberate career advancement strategy rather than a sign of instability.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This trend reflects shifting psychological contracts between employees and organizations, where loyalty is replaced by transactional career optimization. It raises critical questions about whether organizations are failing to provide adequate internal mobility, development, and progression pathways that would retain talent.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should audit their internal career pathing, promotion velocity, and learning opportunities—if high-potential employees feel they must leave to advance, the organization's talent development infrastructure is broken. Building transparent internal mobility programs and accelerated development tracks can reduce costly turnover driven by lily-padding behavior.
