## Summary
U.S. worker productivity has been rising significantly in recent years, driven not by AI but by factors like tight labor markets, digitization, and remote work arrangements.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Tight labor markets force organizations to optimize existing talent rather than simply adding headcount, which can drive process improvements, job redesign, and greater employee autonomy—all well-studied drivers of productivity in organizational psychology.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should recognize that sustainable productivity gains often come from structural investments in work design, digital tools, and flexible work arrangements rather than waiting for transformative technologies. Prioritize optimizing how current employees work before chasing the next technological silver bullet.
