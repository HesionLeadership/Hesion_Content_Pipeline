## Summary
Walmart has identified 10 AI-resistant career paths within its organization and is investing in internal talent development pipelines for these roles, many of which pay six figures, as a strategy to reduce turnover and build employee loyalty.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Walmart's approach reflects strategic internal labor market development—by providing visible career advancement pathways into AI-proof roles, the company leverages expectancy theory (employees invest effort when they see a clear path to valued outcomes) and signals organizational investment in employee growth, which strengthens psychological contracts and reduces voluntary turnover.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should proactively map which roles in their organization will remain essential post-AI disruption and build transparent internal mobility pathways to those roles—this dual strategy addresses both workforce planning and employee retention by giving people a concrete future within the company rather than anxiety about displacement.
