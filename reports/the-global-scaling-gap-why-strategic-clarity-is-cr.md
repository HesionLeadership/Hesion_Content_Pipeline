## Summary
Digital platforms and generative AI are lowering barriers for companies to access global talent, capital, and customers, but research suggests a 'scaling gap' exists where strategic clarity becomes crucial for organizations attempting to leverage these tools for global expansion.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
The tension between technological capability and strategic readiness highlights how organizations must develop clear cognitive frameworks and decision-making structures before scaling globally—without strategic clarity, the democratization of AI tools can lead to organizational fragmentation and misaligned priorities across distributed teams.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should ensure that before adopting AI-driven scaling tools, their organizations have clearly articulated strategic priorities and alignment mechanisms; technology lowers execution barriers but amplifies the cost of strategic ambiguity, making leadership's role in providing direction and coherence more critical, not less.
