# Redefine What ‘Professionalism’ Means

**Source:** MIT Sloan Review  
**Published:** Thu, 25 Jun 2026 11:00:39 +0000

## Summary
The article examines how 'professionalism' norms—such as communication styles, punctuality, meeting etiquette, and camera-on/off expectations—vary widely and argues that organizations should redefine these expectations to be more inclusive and intentional.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Professionalism norms function as implicit organizational culture artifacts that can create exclusion when they reflect dominant-group preferences rather than evidence-based performance standards. Redefining these norms requires surfacing unexamined assumptions and negotiating shared behavioral expectations, which is core to psychological safety and inclusion research.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should facilitate explicit conversations about workplace norms—distinguishing between behaviors that genuinely drive performance and those that merely reflect cultural defaults—to build more inclusive environments and reduce friction from mismatched expectations across diverse teams.
