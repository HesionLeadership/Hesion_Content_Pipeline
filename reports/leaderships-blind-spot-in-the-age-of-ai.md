# Leadership’s Blind Spot in the Age of AI

**Source:** MIT Sloan Review  
**Published:** Tue, 07 Jul 2026 11:00:17 +0000  
**URL:** [https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/leaderships-blind-spot-in-the-age-of-ai/](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/leaderships-blind-spot-in-the-age-of-ai/)

## Summary
MIT Sloan Review article explores how AI is exposing a fundamental blind spot in leadership: the failure to engage in deep, critical thinking about the nature of intelligence, decision-making, and what aspects of cognition should remain human versus be delegated to machines.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Leaders face a metacognitive challenge where the rush to adopt AI tools may bypass the reflective thinking necessary for sound organizational decision-making. This mirrors research on cognitive biases in leadership, where automation complacency and action bias can undermine strategic judgment.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should build organizational capacity for reflective practice and critical thinking alongside AI adoption—creating structured forums where leaders pause to examine which decisions require human judgment, what assumptions are embedded in AI systems, and how automation may be reshaping organizational cognition without deliberate intent.
