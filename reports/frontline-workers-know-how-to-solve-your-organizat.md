## Summary
HBR presents a six-step process for organizations to systematically capture and implement frontline workers' ideas for solving major organizational problems.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Frontline employees possess critical tacit knowledge about operational inefficiencies and customer pain points, yet most organizations lack structured mechanisms to surface and act on this knowledge—leading to disengagement and missed innovation opportunities.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should build formal, iterative systems for soliciting, evaluating, and implementing frontline employee ideas—not just suggestion boxes, but structured processes with feedback loops that signal to employees their input is valued and acted upon, driving both engagement and operational improvement.
