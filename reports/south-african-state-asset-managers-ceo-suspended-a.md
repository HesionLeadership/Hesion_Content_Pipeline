## Summary
The CEO of South Africa's Public Investment Corporation (PIC), a state-owned asset manager, was suspended following a whistleblower report, indicating governance concerns at one of Africa's largest investment managers.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This case highlights the critical role of whistleblower mechanisms in organizational governance and the tension between executive leadership and accountability structures in state-owned enterprises. The suspension suggests the board acted on internal reporting channels, which reflects on organizational justice and ethical climate.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should ensure that whistleblower systems are not only in place but are genuinely functional and credible—boards must demonstrate willingness to act on reports even at the highest levels, as failure to do so erodes trust and ethical culture across the entire organization.
