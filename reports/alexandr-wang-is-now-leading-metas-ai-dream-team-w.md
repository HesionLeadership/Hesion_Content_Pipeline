## Summary
Mark Zuckerberg has appointed Scale AI founder Alexandr Wang to lead Meta's new AI 'dream team' focused on achieving superintelligence, placing an external entrepreneurial leader at the helm of the company's most ambitious internal initiative in a high-stakes race against OpenAI and Google.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This story highlights the organizational dynamics of bringing in an external founder-type leader to drive a critical internal initiative, raising questions about integration, authority, and how incumbent teams respond to an outsider given extraordinary mandate. It also illustrates how organizations construct elite 'tiger teams' to pursue moonshot goals and the psychological pressures of hyper-ambitious framing like 'superintelligence.'

## Leadership Lesson
When appointing external leaders to drive high-stakes internal initiatives, CHROs and COOs must proactively manage status dynamics, ensure role clarity between the new leader and existing executives, and calibrate the ambition narrative—overly grandiose framing can energize top talent but also create burnout and credibility gaps if milestones aren't carefully staged.
