## Summary
Inc article outlines eight practices for designing corporate team offsites that produce lasting strategic outcomes rather than being feel-good events with no follow-through.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Offsites can serve as interventions for team cohesion, psychological safety, and strategic alignment—but only when deliberately designed with clear objectives, structured reflection, and post-retreat accountability mechanisms.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should treat offsites as structured organizational development interventions rather than perks: define measurable outcomes beforehand, create psychologically safe spaces for candid dialogue, and build explicit follow-up processes to translate retreat insights into operational change.
