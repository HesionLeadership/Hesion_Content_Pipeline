# The ‘Job-Hugging’ Trend Is Cracking: Why Half of Employees Are Now Looking to Leave

**Source:** Inc  
**Published:** Mon, 13 Jul 2026 11:17:29 -0400  
**URL:** [https://www.inc.com/bruce-crumley/the-job-hugging-trend-is-cracking-why-half-of-employees-are-now-looking-to-leave/91373100](https://www.inc.com/bruce-crumley/the-job-hugging-trend-is-cracking-why-half-of-employees-are-now-looking-to-leave/91373100)

## Summary
A new study shows that despite the previously 'frozen' labor market where employees were 'job-hugging' (staying put out of fear), 50% of workers are now actively looking to leave their positions, signaling a shift in the balance of power back toward employees.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This reflects a classic cycle in employee retention psychology: when external threat diminishes (economic uncertainty, layoffs), suppressed turnover intention converts to actual job search behavior. Organizations that relied on fear-based retention rather than genuine engagement are now facing a reckoning as employees who stayed involuntarily begin to act on accumulated dissatisfaction.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should recognize that low voluntary turnover during economic uncertainty is not a sign of healthy engagement—it's often masked dissatisfaction. Leaders must proactively invest in re-engagement, career development, and stay conversations now, before the labor market fully thaws and pent-up turnover materializes into costly attrition waves.
