## Summary
The article argues that most leadership teams focus their weekly meetings on accountability and execution metrics, and proposes a single reframing question that shifts meetings toward fostering innovation and organizational learning.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This aligns with research on learning orientation versus performance orientation in teams—organizations that prioritize learning goals over pure performance metrics tend to foster greater psychological safety, experimentation, and long-term adaptability.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should audit their recurring meeting structures to ensure they balance execution accountability with learning-oriented inquiry. Embedding questions like 'What did we learn this week?' or 'What experiment did we run?' can shift team culture from fear-of-failure compliance toward innovation and continuous improvement.
