# A Lawsuit Accuses Meta of Using AI to Lay Off  Workers on Leave. It Reveals a Leadership Mistake Every CEO Should Avoid

## Summary
A lawsuit alleges Meta used AI-driven processes to select employees for layoffs, including those on protected leave, revealing how leadership failures in oversight and human judgment during workforce reductions can create legal and ethical problems.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
When organizations automate high-stakes personnel decisions like layoffs without adequate human oversight, they risk violating employees' sense of procedural and distributive justice, eroding trust and exposing the company to legal liability. The core issue is leadership abdication—delegating morally consequential decisions to algorithms without accountability structures.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs must ensure that any AI-assisted workforce reduction process includes robust human review, particularly for employees in protected categories (e.g., medical leave, parental leave). Algorithmic efficiency cannot replace leadership accountability—leaders must own the decision-making framework and ensure it reflects both legal compliance and organizational values.
