## Summary
Fawn Weaver, founder of Uncle Nearest whiskey, has been ousted from the company she built amid financial difficulties and legal disputes, leaving the company searching for new leadership and a potential buyer.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This case illustrates the classic founder-CEO transition crisis, where the visionary who built the company may lack the operational discipline or stakeholder alignment needed to sustain it through financial turbulence, and where boards must navigate the painful decision to remove a charismatic founder.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should proactively establish governance structures and succession plans that can manage founder transitions before a crisis forces the issue—ensuring the board has clear performance metrics, financial oversight mechanisms, and a leadership pipeline that doesn't rely entirely on one person's identity and vision.
