# A Year After DOGE Cuts, Social Security Is Trying to Stabilize

**Source:** NYT Business RSS  
**Published:** Wed, 15 Jul 2026 09:02:33 +0000  
**URL:** [https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/15/business/social-security-offices.html](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/15/business/social-security-offices.html)

## Summary
After DOGE-driven cuts eliminated about 7,800 staff members from the Social Security Administration, the agency is now quietly rehiring and investing in technology to stabilize operations and manage workloads left by mass departures.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This illustrates the organizational consequences of rapid, large-scale workforce reductions—knowledge loss, operational disruption, and the costly cycle of downsizing followed by rehiring. It also highlights how surviving employees face increased workloads that degrade morale and service quality.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should recognize that aggressive headcount reductions without workforce planning create downstream costs that often exceed the savings. Stabilization after mass cuts requires deliberate knowledge management, technology investment, and rebuilding trust with remaining and newly hired employees.
