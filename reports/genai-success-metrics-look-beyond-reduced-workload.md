# GenAI Success Metrics: Look Beyond Reduced Workload

**Source:** MIT Sloan Review  
**Published:** Wed, 08 Jul 2026 11:00:17 +0000  
**URL:** [https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/genai-success-metrics-look-beyond-reduced-workload/](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/genai-success-metrics-look-beyond-reduced-workload/)

## Summary
A four-year observational study at a large U.S. public higher-education institution found that introducing generative AI tools did not reduce workload or staffing levels, suggesting organizations should look beyond efficiency metrics to measure GenAI success.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
When organizations adopt new technologies expecting workload reduction as the primary outcome, they may overlook more meaningful impacts such as work quality improvements, role enrichment, and cognitive reallocation—phenomena central to job design and sociotechnical systems theory.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should design GenAI success metrics around work quality, employee capability development, and task complexity shifts rather than defaulting to headcount reduction or hours saved, which may never materialize and can undermine adoption motivation.
