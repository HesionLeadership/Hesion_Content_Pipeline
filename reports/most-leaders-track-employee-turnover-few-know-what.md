## Summary
Most leaders track employee turnover rates but fail to understand the full financial cost of each resignation, including hidden expenses beyond recruitment and training.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Turnover costs extend far beyond direct replacement expenses to include lost institutional knowledge, decreased team morale, reduced productivity during transitions, and disrupted social networks—factors that organizational psychology research consistently identifies as critical to team effectiveness.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should move beyond tracking turnover as a simple metric and instead build comprehensive cost models that capture indirect losses (productivity dips, knowledge drain, remaining employee disengagement), using this data to build the business case for targeted retention investments in high-impact roles.
