## Summary
Stanford economist Erik Brynjolfsson argues that the biggest barrier to realizing AI's potential isn't the technology itself, but rather people, organizations, and institutions that need to adapt their structures and mindsets to create shared prosperity.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
The insight that organizational and institutional inertia—not technological capability—is the primary bottleneck for AI adoption reflects core organizational change management principles, including resistance to change, structural rigidity, and the need for redesigning work systems around new technologies.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should stop framing AI as a purely technical implementation challenge and instead invest in organizational redesign, workforce reskilling, and cultural readiness—recognizing that the human and institutional dimensions are the true barriers to capturing AI's value.
