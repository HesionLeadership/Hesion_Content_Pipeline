# Social Security Is Trying to Stabilize a Year After DOGE Cuts

**Source:** NYT Top Stories  
**Published:** 2026-07-15T05:02:33-04:00  
**URL:** [https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/15/business/social-security-offices.html](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/15/business/social-security-offices.html)

## Summary
The Social Security Administration is rebuilding after losing approximately 7,800 staff members due to DOGE-driven cuts, quietly rehiring workers and investing in technology to manage workloads with a diminished workforce.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This illustrates the organizational consequences of rapid, large-scale workforce reductions—surviving employees face increased workloads, morale challenges, and institutional knowledge loss, while leadership must stabilize operations and rebuild trust simultaneously.

## Leadership Lesson
After significant workforce reductions, leaders must prioritize workforce stabilization through strategic rehiring, technology investment to reduce burden on remaining staff, and transparent communication to combat survivor syndrome—cutting too fast without operational planning creates costly recovery cycles that often exceed the original savings.
