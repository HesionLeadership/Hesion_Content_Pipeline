## Summary
The article explores how effective leaders anticipate and manage the emotional reactions their feedback triggers in different employees, offering six techniques to ensure feedback is received constructively.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Feedback effectiveness is moderated by individual differences in emotional reactivity, cognitive appraisal, and feedback orientation. Leaders who tailor their delivery based on anticipated emotional responses can increase feedback acceptance and behavioral change.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should train managers to assess individual employees' feedback receptivity and emotional tendencies before delivering evaluative information, using differentiated approaches rather than one-size-fits-all feedback scripts to maximize developmental impact.
