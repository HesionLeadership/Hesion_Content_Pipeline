## Summary
DBS Bank has embedded innovation into its organizational DNA by making it a KPI worth 20% of every team and individual's performance review, ensuring that innovation is not just a top-down mandate but a distributed responsibility across all levels.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Tying innovation to formal performance metrics signals organizational commitment and shapes employee behavior through goal-setting and accountability mechanisms. This approach leverages expectancy theory—employees are more likely to engage in innovative behavior when they see it is valued, measured, and rewarded.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should consider embedding strategic priorities like innovation directly into performance management systems rather than relying on cultural rhetoric alone. Making innovation a measurable KPI democratizes responsibility and signals that creative problem-solving is everyone's job, not just R&D's.
