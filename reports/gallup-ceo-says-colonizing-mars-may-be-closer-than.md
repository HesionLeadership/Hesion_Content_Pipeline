# Gallup CEO says colonizing Mars may be closer than fixing today’s ‘broken’ workplace—where disengagement levels are as high as 2020

**Source:** Fortune  
**Published:** Tue, 14 Jul 2026 15:03:32 +0000  
**URL:** [https://fortune.com/2026/07/14/gallup-ceo-jon-clifton-workplace-is-broken-mars-colonization-might-come-sooner-artifical-intelligence-anxiety-gen-z/](https://fortune.com/2026/07/14/gallup-ceo-jon-clifton-workplace-is-broken-mars-colonization-might-come-sooner-artifical-intelligence-anxiety-gen-z/)

## Summary
Gallup CEO Jon Clifton reports that global employee disengagement has returned to 2020-level lows, with approximately four in five workers disengaged, and warns that fixing the broken workplace may be harder than colonizing Mars.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This reflects a systemic failure in employee engagement strategies despite decades of research and investment in engagement initiatives. The persistence of disengagement at crisis levels suggests that organizations are either misdiagnosing root causes (e.g., focusing on perks rather than autonomy, meaning, and managerial quality) or failing to implement evidence-based interventions at scale.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should move beyond surface-level engagement programs (surveys without action, wellness perks) and instead invest in manager development, job redesign for autonomy and meaning, and systemic accountability for engagement outcomes at every leadership level. The data suggests that whatever most organizations are currently doing is fundamentally insufficient.
