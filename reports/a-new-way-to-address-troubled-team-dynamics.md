## Summary
Researchers adapted the Big Five personality test into a visual tool for illustrating personality preferences among team members, testing it with over 600 ad hoc teams and 100 senior executive teams to address troubled team dynamics.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Understanding the distribution and interplay of Big Five personality traits within teams can surface hidden sources of conflict, miscommunication, and role misalignment. Making personality differences visible helps teams move from interpersonal friction to constructive dialogue about work styles.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should invest in structured, evidence-based personality mapping within leadership and project teams—not for labeling individuals, but to facilitate transparent conversations about complementary strengths, potential blind spots, and group-level tendencies that drive or derail team effectiveness.
