## Summary
The article argues that founders who believe they are indispensable to daily operations are actually revealing a leadership and organizational design problem, and that taking two weeks off can expose whether a company has built true operational resilience.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This directly addresses founder dependency and the failure to build organizational capacity—a well-studied phenomenon in organizational psychology related to delegation, empowerment, and succession planning. The belief in one's own indispensability often reflects poor structural design and a lack of distributed leadership rather than actual necessity.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should encourage senior leaders to take deliberate, extended absences as a diagnostic tool for organizational health—if the organization struggles without a single person, that signals critical gaps in delegation, role clarity, and leadership pipeline development that must be addressed proactively.
