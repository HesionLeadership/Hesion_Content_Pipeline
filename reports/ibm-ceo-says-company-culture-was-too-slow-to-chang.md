## Summary
IBM CEO discusses how the company's culture was too slow to adapt to changing market conditions, highlighting the challenges of driving cultural transformation in a legacy technology organization.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Cultural inertia in large, established organizations is a well-documented phenomenon where entrenched norms, processes, and mindsets resist change even when leadership recognizes the strategic imperative to evolve. IBM's experience illustrates how legacy cultures can become a competitive liability when the pace of external change outstrips internal adaptation.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should treat culture change as a continuous, proactive discipline rather than a reactive initiative—building mechanisms for ongoing cultural assessment, embedding adaptability into core values, and ensuring leadership at all levels models and reinforces the urgency of transformation before competitive pressures force crisis-driven change.
