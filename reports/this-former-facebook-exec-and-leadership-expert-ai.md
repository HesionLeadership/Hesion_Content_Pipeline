## Summary
A former Facebook executive and leadership expert commits to reading 52 books per year, with the majority being novels, arguing that fiction reading enhances her leadership capabilities.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
Reading literary fiction has been linked to increased empathy and perspective-taking ability (theory of mind), which are foundational competencies for effective leadership, emotional intelligence, and interpersonal dynamics in organizations.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs should consider that leader development isn't confined to business books and formal training—encouraging broad reading, especially fiction, can build empathy, cognitive flexibility, and the perspective-taking skills critical for managing diverse teams and navigating complex stakeholder relationships.
