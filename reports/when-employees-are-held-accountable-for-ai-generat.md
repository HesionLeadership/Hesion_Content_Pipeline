# When Employees Are Held Accountable for AI-Generated Decisions

**Source:** HBR  
**Published:** 2026-07-22T12:05:22Z  
**URL:** [http://feeds.hbr.org/2026/07/when-employees-are-held-accountable-for-ai-generated-decisions](http://feeds.hbr.org/2026/07/when-employees-are-held-accountable-for-ai-generated-decisions)

## Summary
Research reveals that when employees are held accountable for AI-generated decisions, they actively interpret, reshape, and sometimes mask AI outputs to protect their professional credibility and meet stakeholder expectations.

## Organizational Psychology Angle
This highlights a fundamental tension between algorithmic decision-making and individual accountability—employees engage in 'sensemaking' and impression management behaviors when they must own outputs they didn't fully control, creating a facade of human judgment over automated processes.

## Leadership Lesson
CHROs and COOs must redesign accountability structures when deploying AI tools: clarify who owns AI-assisted decisions, create psychological safety for employees to transparently flag when they disagree with AI recommendations, and recognize that without these safeguards, employees will covertly modify or mask AI outputs in ways that undermine both the AI's value and organizational transparency.
